Our Story

Twelve Years of Quiet
Obsession with Craft

Nocturne opened its doors with a single conviction: that dinner should feel like an evening, not a transaction. Every plate begins hours — sometimes days — before it reaches the table, shaped by fire, patience and ingredients sourced from growers we've known for years.

The room is deliberately dim, the service unhurried. We built Nocturne for the kind of evening you remember months later — a particular pour of wine, a dish that changed how you thought about a single vegetable, the low hum of conversation under candlelight.

"Cooking, at its best, is an act of restraint. My job is to know exactly when to stop."

Chef Étienne Roux — Culinary Director

The Kitchen

Chef Étienne Roux

Culinary Director & Founder

Trained in Lyon and later in Tokyo's tsukiji-adjacent kitchens, Chef Roux built his philosophy around restraint — the belief that the finest ingredient needs the least interference. He has led Nocturne's kitchen since its founding, mentoring a brigade now scattered across the world's finest tables.
